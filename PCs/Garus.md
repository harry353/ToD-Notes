Garus was once a soldier. During a retreat, his unit tried to shut a fortress gate, but the hinges were rusted solid. While others panicked, Garus poured oil into the joints and forced it closed, buying time for his comrades to escape. Unbeknownst to him, this simple act of maintenance would set him upon a path of divine purpose. 

That night, he dreamt of Mechanus, the plane of infinite cogs and absolute order. In his vision, a deity appeared; not Primus, or any other god of great renown, but a quiet, overlooked power: Cardea, patron of Hinges, Ball Bearings, Axles, and Cogwheels, also known as the "Ever-Turning". She was not a goddess of conquest or judgment, but of maintenance - a minor divinity born of Mechanus’s endless bureaucracy, tasked with ensuring that the smallest parts kept turning so the great machine would never falter. She spoke with solemn clarity: “Your hinge maintenance has been noted. You have been assigned: lubrication detail. Scope: universal. Objective: what turns must turn well.” 

Since then, Garus has served this oddly specific goddess with unwavering devotion. He blesses hinges, polishes bearings, and treats rust like corruption to be purged. It is expected that a dragonborn paladin would swear to Bahamut, the Platinum Dragon, embodiment of honor and justice. But Garus follows a far smaller, stranger power. To him, the great machine of existence relies not on swords or crowns, but on hinges, bearings, and wheels that turn unseen.

These days, Garus continues his sacred work, traveling from village to village to inspect mills, carts, and gates with the solemnity of a high priest performing ritual. Most take him for an eccentric, but his unwavering dedication caught the attention of an agent of the Order of the Gauntlet. They recruited him not for his sword, but as an example, a living sermon to new initiates on what true devotion looks like. Garus accepted without hesitation, and since then, he has continued to roam the land, seeking out rust, decay, and evil wherever they take root.
