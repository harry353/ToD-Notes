## Session Summary

After the fight, [[Thora]] prays to her god to revive [[Garus]], but receives no response. [[Piiinuz]] suggests placing the body on the altar, which also yields nothing. [[Edgewyn]] sends his bugs to check for vital signs and finds none. Piiinuz takes Garus's form using her shapeshifting. When Edgewyn opens the chest in front of the statue using the key looted earlier, Garus suddenly gasps back to life, to the party's surprise. He remembers nothing beyond [[Frulam Mondath]] killing him. The chest contains a string of pearls, a gold ring with a sapphire, and a small pouch of half a dozen masterfully cut gemstones.

The party scouts nearby rooms before taking a rest, with most being empty. Edgewyn finds a room filled with purple fungi, a large group of sleeping bats, and a few red, fleshy lumps among them. He casts *Speak with Animals* and learns from one of the bats that the lumps are "strange hairless bats." He persuades the bats to guard the party's resting room and scout the adjacent chamber, but the bat sent to scout is killed. The party retreats to the furnished room above and takes a long rest.

Returning to the altar room, [[Garus]] loots [[Cyanwrath]]'s body and takes his greatsword. The party then investigates the room where the bat died, finding a chamber with a ten-foot drop and numerous bodies at the bottom: humans, elves, halflings, and gnomes, all wearing the same plain garments as the prisoners [[Session 8|freed earlier]]. Most appear to have starved to death, with some showing physical wounds and signs of having been gnawed on by non-humanoid teeth.

A staircase in the same room leads down into an immense cavern, warm and humid, with stalactites overhead. At the base of the stairs, iron bars separate the landing from the chamber, with keys hanging beside the door. Beyond the bars, the party makes out large ovular shapes resting on nests: black dragon eggs, which [[Edgewyn]] recognizes as nearly mature. The floor is bloody. Edgewyn fires a dart with *Light* cast on it into a dark corner to illuminate the room, revealing three eggs and a pair of crimson eyes staring back. [[Thora]] and [[Piiinuz]] identify the creature as a roper, a predator that resembles rock and uses tendrils to drag prey toward it.

Before the party can leave, the creature speaks: it asks who they are, what they want, and introduces itself as William. He admits to killing Edgewyn's bat, explains that the cave was originally his, and says the cultists feed him in exchange for guarding the eggs. The party leaves [[William]] for now and moves on.

Passing back through the bat room, the party startles the colony, sending bats swarming around them. Many red, winged creature with beaks and tails attack the party and tries to drain their blood, but they are killed relatively easily, and the party runs. They find a staircase hidden behind hanging pelts and a corner packed with crates, barrels, and household items looted from pillaged villages, including [[Greenest]]. While Edgewyn searches through the pile, the party notices five kobolds creeping up on them.

## Key Events

- [[Garus]] is revived out of nowhere... But at what cost?

- The party discovers a pit room filled with the bodies of starved and partially gnawed prisoners, and descends further into a large cavern housing three mature black dragon eggs guarded by a roper named William, who has a deal with the cultists in exchange for food.
- After the long rest, [[Garus]] loots [[Cyanwrath]]'s greatsword, and the party narrowly escapes the bat colony after red winged creatures try to attack the party, but they are quickly killed.
- The party finds a staircase hidden behind pelts and a cache of looted goods from pillaged villages including [[Greenest]], before noticing five kobolds creeping up on them.
