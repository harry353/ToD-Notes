Wednesday, November 26, 2025

## Session Summary

The party decides to strike the group operating the battering ram. [[Garus]] boosts [[Edgewyn]] onto the roof, and they launch a surprise attack. The rest of the party joins in, and the attackers are quickly defeated. [[Piiinuz]] searches the bodies but finds no additional daggers. [[Garus]] knocks on the temple door to get the townsfolk’s attention, but they only open it after [[Thora]] uses _thaumaturgy_. Several frightened townsfolk peek out and usher the party inside. Most are gathered around the altar, crying or praying, and none appear capable of defending themselves. A single calm [[Eadyan Falconmoon]] stands near a statue of [[Chauntea]]: a robed half-elf priest who speaks with the party about evacuating everyone.

While surveying the room, [[Piiinuz]] notices smoke seeping through the cracks of the back door. After considering their options, the party decides to escort the townsfolk out and [[Piiinuz]] infuses [[Garus]]’s weapon before they depart.

As they try to leave the temple, the earlier patrol arrives, alerted by [[Thora]]’s *thaumaturgy* and the discovery of their slain allies. [[Edgewyn]] is immediately attacked by ambush drakes, and combat breaks out with the panicked townsfolk behind them, though the [[Eadyan Falconmoon|priest]] joins the fight. [[Thora]] kills more than half the attackers with a well-placed _shatter_, and the remaining enemies are finished shortly after, though the priest loses his hand during the fight.

## Key Events

- The party ambushes the group at the battering ram, clears them out, and gains entry to the [[Temple of Chauntea]] after [[Thora]] uses thaumaturgy to convince the frightened townsfolk to open the door.
- Inside, they find terrified civilians and a calm half-elf [[Eadyan Falconmoon|priest]]; [[Piiinuz]] notices smoke coming from the back door, prompting the group to plan an evacuation.
- When they attempt to leave, the earlier patrol confronts them; a fight breaks out, and the party successfully takes them out.
